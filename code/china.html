Famous Mountains & Scenery

China's karst landscapes, such as those in Guilin and Yangshuo, are some of the most breathtaking on Earth. Towering limestone peaks rise above emerald rivers, creating postcard-perfect views at every turn. Take a bamboo raft down the Li River or hike through these iconic formations to experience their full magic.

Tradition & Rural Culture

Meet the cormorant fishermen of southern China, a tradition that dates back over a thousand years. These local fishers use trained birds to catch fish along calm rivers, often at dawn or dusk. It's a living heritage that's not only impressive to witness but also deeply symbolic of China's connection with nature.

Climate Overview

Southern China has a subtropical climate with hot, humid summers and mild winters. Spring and autumn are ideal for travel, offering comfortable weather and fewer tourists. In the mountainous regions, mist often rolls through the valleys in the early morning, adding a mysterious atmosphere perfect for photographers and nature lovers.
